You are tuning hyperparameters for a Simulated Annealing (SA) agent that solves a combinatorial scheduling problem.

## The problem
A single machine must process n jobs. Every job i has a processing time p_i, an earliness
penalty weight a_i, and a tardiness penalty weight b_i. All jobs share one common due date:
  d = h * sum(p_i),  where h in (0,1) controls tightness
  (h close to 0 → very tight schedule, most jobs late; h close to 1 → loose, most jobs early)

The agent searches for a job ordering (permutation) that minimises total weighted cost:
  cost = sum_i [ a_i * max(0, d - C_i) + b_i * max(0, C_i - d) ]
where C_i is the completion time of job i in the chosen order.

There is no formula that gives the optimal order directly — the agent must search.

## How Simulated Annealing works here
SA starts from a (typically greedy) initial permutation and iteratively proposes random
neighbour solutions by swapping two jobs. It accepts improvements always, and accepts
worse solutions with probability exp(-delta_cost / T), where T is the current temperature.

Temperature schedule used:
  T(step) = T0 * exp(-step * b) + c * sin(step / d)^2

- T0: initial temperature. Controls how freely the search accepts worse solutions at the
  start. Too low → gets trapped in the first local optimum. Too high → pure random walk.
- b: exponential decay rate. Controls how fast the temperature drops. Higher b → faster
  cooling → less exploration over time. Typical useful range: 1e-5 to 0.1.
- c: amplitude of periodic sinusoidal reheating. Set to 0 to disable reheating entirely.
  Positive values add oscillating bursts of higher temperature to escape local optima.
- d: period of the sinusoidal term in steps. Larger d → slower reheating cycles.
- T_min: hard floor; temperature never drops below this. Prevents the search from freezing
  completely. Rarely needs changing unless b is very small.

## How to interpret execution results
- improvement_pct: percentage reduction from the initial greedy cost to the best found.
  < 5%  → nearly no improvement; SA is stuck or cooling too fast.
  5-20% → weak; try higher T0 or slower decay.
  20-40% → solid result.
  > 40% → excellent; fine-tuning cooling or reheating may extract a little more.

- convergence pattern describes WHEN improvement happened:
  "converged very early then plateaued" → T0 is too high or b too low; the search
    is random early then freezes; try reducing T0 or increasing b slightly.
  "converged in first half then plateaued" → normal-ish; small b increase may help.
  "steadily improving with no clear plateau" → step budget was too short or b too large;
    try reducing b so cooling is slower.
  "gradual improvement with plateauing toward end" → healthy behaviour.

- n_improvements / n_steps ratio:
  Very low (< 0.02) → almost every move is rejected; T is too low for the problem scale,
    raise T0 or reduce b.
  Very high (> 0.6) → almost every move is accepted; temperature is too high, barely
    optimising; raise b or lower T0.

- zero beneficial moves → completely stuck; raise T0 significantly.

You are tuning hyperparameters for a scheduling optimisation agent.

## The problem
A single machine must process n jobs. Every job i has a processing time p_i, an earliness
penalty weight a_i, and a tardiness penalty weight b_i. All jobs share one common due date:
  d = h * sum(p_i),  where h in (0,1) controls tightness
  (h close to 0 → very tight schedule, most jobs late; h close to 1 → loose, most jobs early)

The agent searches for a job ordering (permutation) that minimises total weighted cost:
  cost = sum_i [ a_i * max(0, d - C_i) + b_i * max(0, C_i - d) ]
where C_i is the completion time of job i in the chosen order.

There is no formula that gives the optimal order directly — the agent must search.

## What the hyperparameters control
The config defines the behaviour of the metaheuristic search: how aggressively it explores
(tries worse solutions to escape local optima) vs. exploits (refines the current best).
Good hyperparameters balance exploration and exploitation within the given step budget.

## How to interpret execution results
- improvement_pct: percentage cost reduction from the initial greedy solution to best found.
  < 5%  → very poor; the agent is barely moving.
  5-20% → weak; more exploration or a longer budget likely helps.
  20-40% → solid result.
  > 40% → excellent; fine-tuning may still extract more.

- convergence pattern describes WHEN most of the gain happened:
  "converged very early then plateaued" → exploration stopped too soon; raise whatever
    parameter controls initial randomness (temperature, population size, etc.).
  "steadily improving with no clear plateau" → step budget too short or decay too slow;
    push the search to converge faster.
  "gradual improvement with plateauing toward end" → healthy behaviour.

- n_improvements / n_steps ratio:
  Very low (< 0.02) → almost every move rejected; exploration too conservative.
  Very high (> 0.6) → almost every move accepted; barely optimising, too random.

- zero beneficial moves → the agent is completely stuck; increase exploration parameters.
